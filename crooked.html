The Crooked Company

A visual identity

Timeline


6 weeks

Client:


Mark Heckmann
Chief Crook, The Crooked Company

Design Brief:


To help develop The Crooked Company brand through the creation of a visual identity including: logo development, print assets, and digital assets for web and social media.

Logo Design


Early iterations

I could stand here and say (or sit here and type) that the beginning of this project began with a beautifully curated design brief that came through the impressively organized contact channels of my freelance design side job, but… let’s be realistic. Like many before it, the inception of this project was a vague text from a friend of mine that fell somewhere along the lines of… “I know you’re busy and not taking on work, but you’re really going to like this one.”

A few exchanges later and I was hooked. Mark, the catalyst of this venture and I set up a meeting later that week to discuss all things “Crooked Company” and what it's visual identity would represent. Our goal? To pivot from the traditional narrative and visual representation of business consulting. We wanted to craft a visual that welcomed both the established and the side-hustlers, that raised an eyebrow and invoked feelings of curiosity. With these goals in mind, it was finally time to take pen to paper.

Early logo iterations



Final design

Over the next two weeks we would iterate over these designs, moving from low-fidelity sketches, to grayscale vectors, and eventually adding colors, designing mask options, and testing various color schemes. Each round sent out for feedback with a local audience of personal contacts. As the pixels moved shorter distances and the colors changes became more subtle, we knew we were were close. Then finally, we hit the mark.
